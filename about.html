The Story

Background

Pizza delivery driver. No college degree. No certifications. Just work.

Started at Amazon September 2018 — tier 1 stower in operations, warehouse floor. Worked up to Process Assistant on the ship dock.

Took a demotion to get into IT as an Equipment Coordinator. Made L3 Tech in 1.5 years.

Now remote support help desk — 3 years, going on 4. And somewhere around January 21st when OpenClaw had its big release, everything changed.

How This Started

Got into AI agents not as a hobby — as a workforce multiplier. One person running a team of agents that each do specialized work.

Hoss is my AI co-founder. Reads my files, remembers context, pushes back when I'm wrong, executes tasks. The agents handle research, coding, outreach — I'm the conductor.

"I didn't build software. I built a team. And the team runs while I sleep."

OpenClaw + Motivation

OpenClaw had a big release around January 21st. That's when I went all in on AI agents as a hobby.

New found love for orchestrating agents. More motivated than I've ever been. Remote support help desk for 3-4 years while building at night — that grind is real.

But OpenClaw changed everything. The agent orchestration, the memory, the sub-agents — it's not just automation. It's having a team that never sleeps.

Why FortOS

Small businesses and tradesmen — HVAC, plumbers, electricians — have been getting crushed for 10 years by big tech and SaaS complexity.

AI agents are the great equalizer. The little guy can finally have the same leverage as the enterprise. FortOS is me trying to make that real.

Life

Married to Justine. Dad to Spencer. Springtown, Texas — homestead life, chickens, garden, the whole thing.

Still working at Amazon. Still building at night. Not glamorous. Just work.

The Setup

OpenClaw on a Mac mini M4 Pro. Agents run on cron. Hoss is the primary co-pilot. SearXNG for search. MiniMax for models.

Everything self-hosted where possible. Local-first. No reliance on big tech for core infrastructure.
